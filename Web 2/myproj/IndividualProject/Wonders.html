This page helps highlight some of the natural wonders of the park

General Sherman Tree

The General Sherman Tree is the world's largest tree (known to the public). Standing at an impressive 275ft tall and with a trunk diameter of 7.7m. I have see this tree in person and it is amazing at how big yet how gentle it looks. There is another tree taller than the General Sherman Tree but that is a well kept secret within the park rangers as they don't want the public to disturb the ecological environment its in since that tree is located in a remote area.



Moro Rock

Moro Rock is a granite dome located at the center of the park between the Giant Forest and the Crescent Meadow. A 400 step stairway leads you to the top but this is closed during lightning storms. The stairway is listed on the National Registar of Historic Places. It gives a spetacular view of the park at 6,725ft.



Crescent Meadow

Crescent Meadow was discovered by John Muir who called it the "Gem of the Sierras". It truly is nature in it's pristine beauty. There are a lot of trails that lead to crescent meadow and allow you to take in the natural beauty around this gem.



Giant Forest

When I first stepped into the Giant Forest I felt like I had ventured into the forbidden forest from Harry Potter. I was speechless as I looked out the window of my car as we neared the forest. All I saw was giant tree's some whose trunk size was bigger than my room. It was pitch black inside the forest as light struggles to penetrate beyond these sleeping giants. This is something to see at least once in your lifetime as there is nothing else on earth than can compare to these tree's.
