Wildlife of Sequioa National Park

There is a vast amount of wildlife in the mountains of the Sierra. These are just some of animals that I have encountered in my journey through the park.

Black Bears

The most interesting animal in the park and also the deadliest, the black bear. The first thing you are warned about as you step into the park is to not feed black bears as they will get violent once they get used to being fed by humans. Generally they are a peaceful bunch but they are interested in exploring new things so black bears frequently visit camp sites. I once saw a litter of cubs followed by the mama bear and they sure are a playful bunch but don't feed them.



Grizzly Bear

The symbol on California's flag once abundant in the Sierra mountains is now extinct due to hunters. The last California grizzly bear was seen in Sequoia Park in 1924. They would prey on the livestock of settlers and for that reason they were hunted and captured by Europeans and put in fights against bulls as a spectator sport.



Marmot

Apparently the Marmot is very common sight although I have not seen one in the park. They are very playful and you can hear their whistles in Crescent Meadow.



Big Horn Ram

I have only seen these once and the only reason I saw them was because we were camping at a high altitude that year because all the lower camp sites were taken. I don't like these guys at all they are NOT friendly and have no problem charging at you if they feel threatened.
