Death Valley National Park

Death Valley National Park is another park that is near Sequoia National Park and is east of both Yosemite and Sequoia Park. In contrast to both parks it actually lives up to its name and has a beauty of its own. It occupies both California and parts of Nevada.



Sand dunes are a frequent sight at the park as amazing shapes are sculpted by the winds in the park. The dunes are a piece of work and each one as unique as a snowflake.



The badwater basin is below sea level and it is the hottest area in North America. It recieves less that 1.5 inches of rainfall annually. Temperatures here can reach excess of 120 F and can reach 20 F during the winter.
