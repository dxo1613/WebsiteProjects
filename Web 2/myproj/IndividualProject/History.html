History of Sequoia National Park


Sequoia National Park is America's second oldest national park. Western Mono Native Americans settled parts of the park traveling over the mountains on trade routes. In the 1860's European settlers arrived and settled there as farmers.



The park is home to five of the ten world's largest trees in the fabled Giant Forest. It is also the home of the General Sherman Tree which it the largest living single stem tree in the world standing at an impressive 275 feet tall.
